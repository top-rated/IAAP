Constitution

CONSTITUTION OF THE UKRAINIAN DEVELOPMENT GROUP OF THE INTERNATIONAL ASSOCIATION OF ANALYTICAL PSYCHOLOGY

Adopted at the General Meeting of the UGR on September 16, 2008

With changes approved at the General Meeting of the UGR on February 6, 2010

With changes approved at the General Meeting of the UGR on July 3, 2011

With changes approved at the General Assembly of the UGR on February 5, 2016

Article 1. Status, name and location.

1.1 The Ukrainian development group of the International Association of Analytical Psychology (IAAP) (hereinafter UGR) is an association of people interested in and developing analytical psychology K.G. Boy. It acts in accordance with the IAAP Constitution and the Laws of Ukraine.

1.2 Full name:

Ukrainian development group of the International Association of Analytical Psychology (IAAP).

Name in English: Ukrainian Developing Group of International Association of Analytical Psychology.

1.3 Address: I. Kudri 31 square 26

Article 2. Purpose and tasks of UGR.

2.1 The goal of UGR is to create a community of Jungian analysts in Ukraine within the framework of the IAAP.

2.2 Tasks of UGR:

- to organize the training of Jungian analysts with the assistance of the IAAP

- To spread knowledge of Jungian psychoanalysis and psychotherapy.

- Pursue adherence to high standards of training, practice and professional ethics of Jungian analysts and psychotherapists.

- Cooperate with international and national organizations in the field of analytical psychology, psychoanalysis and psychotherapy.

Article 3. Membership in UGR, rights and obligations of members.

3.1 Membership in the Ukrainian Development Group can only be individual. Citizens of Ukraine, foreign citizens and stateless persons can be members of UGR.

3.2 There are three categories of membership in UGR:

– valid member;

– associate member;

– an associate member, entitled to choose and be elected to the UGR Council

3.3 Current members of the UGR.

3.3.1 Associate members who have fulfilled the following requirements become valid members:

– individual members of the IAAP or associate members who have successfully passed the selection in the training program of the IAAP (Router) or other communities within the IAAP to obtain the status of an individual member of the IAAP;

3.3.2 Rights and obligations.

An active member of the UGR has the right to:

- participate in general meetings and vote;

– elect and be elected to the UGR Council;

- conduct interviews and give recommendations to those wishing to enter the UGR;

- participate in training programs, seminars, group supervisions and discussion groups to discuss clinical cases;

- participate in events organized by UGR;

An active member of the UGR is obliged to:

- to improve one's professional qualifications in analytical psychology and psychotherapy through active participation in training and educational programs organized by UGR or IAAP;

- pay membership fees.

3.4 Associate members of the UGR

3.4.1 Any person who shows interest in the analytical psychology of K.G. can become an associate member of the UGR. Boy. To become an associate member of the UGR, he/she must (a) apply to the UGR secretary, obtain recommendations from two members of the UGR, including one active member, and pass an introductory interview with two members of the UGR, including one active member . The decision on acceptance into associate members of the UGR is made based on the results of the interview at the General Meeting of the development group by a simple majority of the valid members of the UGR.

3.4.2 Rights and obligations.

An associate member has the right to:

- participate in general meetings of the UGR (without the right to elect and be elected to the UGR council);

- participate in seminars, group supervisions and discussion groups to discuss clinical cases;

- apply for valid membership of the UGR;

- to propose their materials for publication on the topics of analytical psychology and psychotherapy

- to receive information about all events of the UGR.

An associate member of the UGR is obliged to:

- pay membership fees.

3.5 Associate members of the UGR, entitled to elect the UGR council and be elected.

3.5.1 An associate member of the UGR may be granted the right to vote in the elections to the UGR council, if he submits a statement to the secretary about his desire to participate in the elections and is approved at the next general meeting of the UGR by the votes of valid members of the UGR. The main criterion for approval is active participation in the activities of the UGR. Only active members of the UGR have the right to be elected to the post of President of the UGR.

3.6 UGR members must recognize the IAAP code of ethics and the UGR constitution and act in accordance with them.

3.7 Termination of membership in UGR.

Membership in the UGR may be terminated or suspended by a decision of the General Meeting under the following circumstances:

- on the basis of a written application for withdrawal from the UGR or suspension of membership submitted to the secretary of the UGR;

– in case of death of a member of the group;

– in case of serious violation of professional and ethical standards;

– in case of non-payment of membership fees for a period exceeding 12 months;

Article 4. The structure of the governing bodies of the Ukrainian Development Group.

4.1 The activity of the UGR is carried out on a democratic basis.

4.2 The highest governing body of the UGR is the general meeting of the UGR members (hereinafter - the General Meeting of the UGR). It is held at least twice a year in terms announced no later than two weeks in advance.

4.3 Extraordinary general meetings may be convened by the UGR council or at the request of a third or more of the active members of the UGR.

4.4 The quorum for decision-making is at least 2/3 of the members of the UGR with the right to vote. It is allowed to transfer the votes of those absent at the meeting to those present upon written application of the former

4.5 The range of issues that must be considered at the General Meeting.

When resolving such issues, they are adopted by a majority of two-thirds of the votes of persons entitled to vote

- changes in the constitution of the UGR

- dissolution of the UGR

- exclusion from the membership of the UGR

4.5 A simple majority (half of the voting members plus one vote) is sufficient for making decisions on other issues. In particular, the following issues are resolved at the UGR General Meeting:

- approval of the financial report of the UGR treasurer, the annual report of the UGR President and council members

– elections of the President of the UGR and members of the UGR Council

- amount and terms of payment of membership fees

4.6 The executive body of the UGR is the Council of the UGR.

4.6.1 The Council consists of the President, Vice-President, Secretary and Treasurer. Each member of the council is elected for a term of three years with possible further re-election. The next (third) election is allowed only after a three-year break.

4.6.2 The Council manages the current activities of the UGR and implements the decisions made at the UGR General Meeting.

4.6.3 The Council distributes responsibilities and gives instructions for the performance of tasks within the framework of the activities of the UGR.

4.6.4 The Council holds its meetings at least once every three months.

4.6.5 Duties of the President of the UGR:

- organization and control of the administrative activities of the UGR Council

- distribution of responsibilities among the members of the UGR Council

- making decisions in the interests of the UGR

- The President represents the UGR and acts on its behalf and in its interests

- initiates the implementation of new UGR projects

- informs members of the UGR about IAAP decisions related to the activities of the UGR

4.6.6 Duties of the Vice-President of the UGR:

– organization of current activities of UGR

– management of current UGR projects

- performs the duties of the President in his absence

4.6.7 Duties of the secretary of the UGR:

- informing members of UGR about current activities of the Council and UGR projects

- maintaining contacts with IAAP and other partners of UGR

- keeping the minutes of the General Meeting of the UGR

4.6.8. Duties of the UGR treasurer:

- keep records of all financial funds and property of UGR;

- to report on financial calculations and the state of ownership at the annual general meeting of the UGR;

- redistribute funds within the approved budget;

4.7 The audit commission of the UGR is elected from active members by the General Assembly, consisting of the chairman of the commission and two of his members, for a term of 3 years.

4.7.1 The President and members of the UGR Council cannot be members of the Audit Commission.

4.7.2 The audit commission controls the spending of UGR funds.

4.7.3 The audit committee has the right to: submit to the General Meeting any issues related to the results of its audits.

4.8 The procedure for the formation and activity of the Ethics Commission is determined at the General Meeting of the UGR and is regulated by its Regulations.

Article 5. Educational and educational activities of the UGR

Educational and educational activities of UGR are implemented through the system of UGR projects. The responsible person in the project system is the Vice President of the UGR. In the UGR project, the responsible person is the coordinator, who ensures the life of the project. For supervision projects and analysis projects, the coordinator should not be a direct participant in the project.

Article 5. Property and finances.

5.1 UGR is the owner of its property.

5.2 The property of the UGR consists of equipment, things, publications, books, information, funds, websites and any other legally acquired property necessary for the activities of the UGR and its members.

5.3 UGR members do not have the right to own a share of property belonging to the group, and do not retain the rights to the property transferred by them to the group, including annual membership fees.

5.4 The sources of formation of funds and property of UGR are:

– introductory and annual membership fees of UGR members;

- voluntary contributions and donations of legal entities and individuals and other sources not prohibited by law;

- income from holding conferences, seminars and other events organized by the UGR.

5.5 Incomes and funds of UGR can be used to cover current expenses of UGR, organization of seminars and conferences of UGR.

5.6 The UGR Council is responsible for financial accounting in accordance with the procedure established by the UGR General Assembly.

Article 6. Representation of the UGR.

6.1 The UGR is represented by the President, who is empowered to act on his own behalf in all matters concerning the UGR. He can appoint another member of the Council of UGR to perform similar powers instead of him.

6.2 Any documents that have legal force are signed by the president and one of the members of the UGR council.

Article 7. Termination of UGR activities

7.1 Termination of the activity of the UGR is carried out through its reorganization or liquidation.

7.2 The reorganization of the UGR is carried out in accordance with this Constitution. Upon reorganization of UGR, all its rights and obligations are transferred to its legal successor.

7.3 Liquidation of the UGR is carried out on the basis of this Constitution or a court decision. In case of liquidation of the UGR, the General Assembly of the UGR shall establish the procedure and terms of the liquidation.

7.4 Upon liquidation of the UGR, all of its property, after settlement of obligations, shall be directed by the decision of the UGR General Meeting to the fulfillment of statutory tasks or charitable purposes related to the objectives of the UGR.

7.5 Funds and other property of the UGR cannot be redistributed among the members of the UGR.
