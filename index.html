ABOUT US

Ukrainian IAAP Development Group

The Ukrainian IAAP development group was created for the purpose of professional development of psychologists and psychotherapists and the organization of training in analytical psychology according to international standards. Analytical psychology originated in the works of the Swiss psychiatrist Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961).

Jungian method

The goal of Jungian analysis is what Jung called individuation. Individuation should not be confused simply with individuality or eccentricity. Rather, individuation means achieving a better understanding of the factors that influence how a person interacts with the totality of his psychological, interpersonal, and cultural experiences. Jung defined two deep levels of psychological functioning, which mainly shape, color and sometimes negatively affect the life experience of a person.

The analytical process is aimed at bringing both personal and collective factors to the level of consciousness, which will allow a person to see more clearly what forces play their role in his life. Jung's understanding of archetypes in particular involves a sense of "telos" or purpose toward which life can be directed. The analyst's role is to facilitate the individuation process and accompany the analysand on his personal journey.
